Case Study

IntelliShop

A mobile shopping assistant designed around real household pain points: forgetting what is already at home, overspending, poor stock visibility and the effort of checking prices across multiple supermarkets.

Problem

Existing shopping apps often support basic list creation, but many do not combine intelligent stock awareness, budget-based shopping support, local price comparison and recipe guidance in a coherent, user-friendly experience.

Goal

Design an AI-supported mobile product that helps users plan shopping more efficiently, reduce waste, improve decision-making and make everyday grocery management feel simpler and clearer.

UX/UI Designer & Developer Expo Figma AI Features

My role

I worked across the full process: problem framing, research synthesis, interface planning, wireframing, prototyping, screen design and implementation translation into a functional mobile application.

I treated the project as both a UX challenge and a real product build, which meant aligning interface decisions with how the app would actually function in development.

Focused on simple task flows, clear information hierarchy and feature grouping that supported confidence rather than overload.

Implementation

Translated the concept into a functional app, balancing UX goals with real development constraints across navigation, forms and product structure.

UX thinking

I treated budget suggestions, inventory visibility and price comparison as connected decision-making problems rather than isolated features, helping the product feel more coherent and useful.

Tech

React Native, Expo, Firebase and external APIs supported the live product direction, allowing the case study to move beyond concept visuals into a functional experience.

Outcome

The result was a clearer, more structured shopping assistant that improved feature discoverability, supported personalised decisions and demonstrated how UX design can directly shape implementation.

Case Study

AI Bedtime Story Teller

A responsive storytelling platform designed to help parents create personalised bedtime stories with AI, convert them into audio and provide children with a safe, simplified playback experience.

Problem

Parents want meaningful bedtime routines, but do not always have the time or energy to write original stories. Children, meanwhile, benefit from personalised and engaging content that feels familiar, simple and emotionally relevant.

Goal

Design a responsive platform that makes story creation easier for parents while keeping the child experience safer, simpler and focused purely on playback and enjoyment.

My role

I worked on UX/UI design and product structure, including personas, empathy mapping, journey mapping, task analysis, user flows, wireframes, responsive interface design and implementation direction, while also producing a live functional web version.

Why this matters

This project demonstrates not just visual design, but UX reasoning: multi-user journeys, role-based permissions, content creation flows, information hierarchy and responsive behavior across devices.

01

Research

Defined user needs around bedtime routines, personalization, emotional connection and child-safe digital interaction.

02

Personas

Created parent and child personas such as Ana, Carl, Lucas and Michael to shape the product around motivations, goals and frustrations.

03

User flows

Mapped end-to-end parent and child journeys, from sign-up and story creation to playback, favourites and story reuse.

04

Build

Translated the concept into a responsive Next.js and Firebase-based product with AI-assisted content generation and text-to-speech direction.

UX thinking

A major design decision was separating the parent and child experiences. Parents needed full control over story creation, editing and management, while children needed a simplified interface focused only on browsing and playback.

Tech

The product architecture was designed around React and Next.js on the frontend, Firebase for authentication and Firestore, AI APIs for content generation and text-to-speech services for audio playback.

Outcome

The final result is a live responsive website supported by a complete UX process, showing my ability to move from user research and interaction planning into practical product delivery.
