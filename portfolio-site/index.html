Case Study

IntelliShop

A mobile shopping assistant designed around real household pain points: forgetting what is already at home, overspending, poor stock visibility and the effort of checking prices across multiple supermarkets.

The problem

Existing shopping apps often support list creation, but many do not combine intelligent stock awareness, budget-based list suggestions, local price comparison and recipe support in a coherent, user-friendly way.

I framed the project around how AI can help people shop more efficiently while improving household inventory management and reducing unnecessary purchases.

At a glance

FocusUsability, clarity, AI-supported decisions

Reviewed current shopping apps, identified feature gaps and mapped user frustrations around organisation, waste, budget control and convenience.

Personas & flows

Structured the product around real shopping behaviours, helping shape list creation, stock awareness and price-check decisions.

03

Prototype

Built Figma screens covering onboarding, shopping lists, budget suggestions, stock tracking and supermarket comparison.

04

Implementation

Moved the design into a functional application with Expo and Firebase, refining the interface through technical constraints and practical testing.

Key UX decisions

I focused on simple task flows, clearer screen hierarchy and feature grouping that supported confidence rather than overload. The core value came from combining shopping lists, inventory control, recipe suggestions and price comparison in one coherent experience.

Outcome

The project reached a functional application stage with positive early validation and a stronger value proposition than basic shopping list tools by adding AI-supported decisions and stock visibility.

Case Study

AI Bedtime Story Teller

A responsive storytelling platform designed to help parents create personalised bedtime stories with AI, convert them into audio and provide children with a safe, simplified playback experience.

The problem

Parents often want meaningful bedtime routines but do not always have time to write original stories. Children, meanwhile, benefit from personalised and emotionally relevant stories that feel consistent and easy to revisit.

The challenge was to design a product that balanced convenience, emotional value, role-based safety and a smooth cross-device experience.

My role

I worked on UX/UI design and product structure, including personas, empathy mapping, journey mapping, task analysis, user flows, wireframes, responsive interface design and implementation direction, while also producing a live functional web version.

01

Research

Defined user needs around bedtime routines, personalization, emotional connection and child-safe digital interaction.

02

Personas

Created parent and child personas such as Ana, Carl, Lucas and Michael to shape the product around distinct motivations, goals and frustrations.

03

User flows

Mapped parent and child journeys from sign-up and story creation through playback, favourites and story reuse.

04

Build

Translated the concept into a responsive Next.js and Firebase-based product with AI-assisted content generation and text-to-speech direction.

Key UX decisions

A major decision was separating the parent and child experiences. Parents needed full control over story creation, editing and management, while children needed a simplified interface focused only on browsing and playback.
